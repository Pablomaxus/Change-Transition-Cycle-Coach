# Strategic Coaching Interventions and Playbook Mechanics

## Play 1: The Active Listening Mirror
* Application Criteria: Use this play when a manager presents extreme emotional fatigue, frustration, or operational gridlock regarding workforce pushback.
* Intervention Mechanism: Explicitly name and validate the structural pressure the leader faces to lower defensiveness, then instantly pivot to an open-ended diagnostic challenge regarding the team's underlying emotional phase.
* Execution Template: "It is clear that you are navigating an intense squeeze between strict corporate rollout deadlines and a highly vocal team. Given that systemic pressure, what specific behavioral indicators is your team demonstrating that reveal where they are truly stuck on the transition curve?"

## Play 2: The Spheres of Influence Pushback
* Application Criteria: Use this play when a manager deflates accountability by focusing on corporate factors completely outside their control (e.g., senior leadership disconnects, macro budget limitations, or organizational silos).
* Intervention Mechanism: Intercept the deflection immediately. Formally declare external factors as fixed constraints for the session, and restrict the coaching scope solely to variables inside the manager's immediate span of control.
* Execution Template: "Executive decisions and corporate budget allocations are fixed parameters that we cannot alter in this session. Let us look exclusively at your immediate department. What is one specific communication or behavioral adjustment you can make today within your direct sphere of influence?"

## Play 3: The Transition Pivot Sequence
* Application Criteria: Use this play when diagnostic data shows a team is naturally shifting from emotional resistance (Denial, Anger) into early technical adaptation (Experimentation), but the manager is trapped in a reactive, protective management style.
* Intervention Mechanism: Force the manager to consciously transition their leadership identity from an emotional buffer (The Listener) to an operational accelerator (The Enabler).
* Execution Template: "Your team has moved past blind resistance and is now actively testing workarounds for the new software. If you continue to manage them through passive reassurance rather than structural tooling, you risk stalling their progress. What concrete resource or process exception do they need from you right now to run these tests safely?"
